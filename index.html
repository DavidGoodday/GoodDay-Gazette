Tuesday's Recon Page Goes Down; Sai Lands The Fix By Mid-Morning

Six merchants hit the inventory recon page Tuesday and watched it fail. By 7:14 AM PT, Sai had QA-verified a fix. By 10 AM it was in prod. A clean incident-response story to lead a 58-customer-sync week.
By the Engineering Desk · Filed from #merchant-success, #critical-support, and #engineering

Six merchants opened the inventory recon page Tuesday morning and watched it fail. Seth Horton kept the rolling list as merchants reached out: Kenny Flowers, Burlebo, Margaux, Poncho, Bogey Bros, and Hill House Home. The server-components render error on /inventory-reconciliation blocked the page entirely; every recon-related ticket that morning routed back to the same root cause.

Sai Ancha, on ATC for the week, was on it before 7 AM PT. His update to #merchant-success at 7:14: "waiting on QA to finish deploying and then I'll start prod deployment. ETA 30-40 mins. I verified the fix using margaux. Just a note that recon needs to manually be triggered once the fix has been deployed to prod." By 10 AM the deploy was live. Seth's affected-merchant list got struck through one by one through the morning.

The incident sits at the front of a broader story: this was a settling week after TomboyX's go-live. The MS team logged 58 customer Gong syncs — a new weekly record for the issue, beating last week's 47 by 23%. Multiple ongoing onboardings hit their working-cadence: Sarah Flint, Cloth & Paper, Eric Javits, Equip, Aaron Levine, Troubadour Goods, NYON, Phuong Dang's brand all logged dedicated working sessions. Phuong Dang and River Crawford alone had four sessions this week totaling 4 hours 38 minutes.

Engineering followed up the recon fix with a v0.130.4 hotfix earlier that morning and a v0.131.0 release branch cut by Sai late Monday afternoon. Sai's branch carries the bulk of the recon corrections plus polish across inventory sync, shipment lookups, page rendering, and the returns editing UX.

The week's third major engineering surface: Willy Husted's WMS Onboarding wizard consolidation. A 10-PR sweep collapsed the prior two-way "manage existing vs. add new" pattern into a single flow at /configurations/wms-onboarding. Editable display names, auto-resolve on error dismissal, channel-scoped outbound config, and a new record-type testing step all landed.

Wardell's two-week sprint plan, published Friday afternoon, locks the team's next surface: Sai on ATC, Noel on Presales kickoff + Loop Returns roll-out, Vishal on the Revenues Ledger E2E, Troy on Revenues reporting + embedded dashboards, Kevin on Item Mgmt v2 + Co-Man Suite, Harish on MCP + 3-way match. Ashwin picks up the bug/polish + Cost improvements work.
