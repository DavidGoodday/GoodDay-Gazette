GoodDay Turns Its AI Inward: A Support Agent Goes Live and "Aurora" Joins the Team

The same week the team demoed its Claude-powered platform to prospects, it quietly put two of its own agents to work — one triaging support tickets, one briefing Merchant Success.
By the Gazette Desk · Reporting July 10 – 16, 2026

For months GoodDay has sold merchants on building with AI. This week it turned the lens around. Hunter Leeming shipped V1 of an internal AI Support Agent: when a Pylon ticket is created, the agent takes 10–40 seconds to draft an answer from the knowledge base, drops an internal note on the ticket, and posts to a new #project-support-ai-agent channel for an MSM to review. Nothing goes to a merchant automatically — a human copies, confirms sources, and sends. "We'll keep the agent in this manual mode until we feel comfortable with the responses it is generating," Leeming wrote.

Days later, Kyle Hency introduced "Aurora," an AI assistant given enough context to surface the week's key highlights across the most important accounts, now pulled into the Merchant Success squad in a merch-facing form. The team even held a vote on her app icon — with River helping find options that ChatGPT hadn't "overly sexualized."

Both land on top of the workhorse the team already leans on daily, Ser Rekonalot, whose ticket volume this week ran hotter than ever. The message is consistent: GoodDay wants its own people to get the same leverage it keeps pitching to brands — build with the agents, review their work, and keep a human in the loop.

It made for a fitting headline in a week that also brought a marquee demo and a heavier-than-usual support load.

Where the Roadmap Gets Built

Canopy's Case-Level BOM Forces a Modeling Decision

How to count 20 date-code labels and one master carton per case becomes the week's sharpest product question — while reporting, returns, and the data warehouse all push forward.
Sources: six active project channels

Item management had the meatiest debate. Canopy ships in cases of 18, but two components don't map one-to-one: date-code labels (18 on gift boxes + 2 on the master carton = 20 per case) and the carton itself (1 per 18 units). Both are inventory-tracked and drive real POs, so the count must be exact. Kevin and Dave weighed two paths — Path 1, a case-aware "extra units per case" property on the UOM conversion (most semantically correct, more engineering), versus Path 2, fractional per-unit BOM quantities like 20/18 of a label per unit (fastest, matches Canopy's current spreadsheet, but rounding-sensitive for cartons). The likely call: Path 2 to unblock Canopy now, Path 1 as the longer-term model. A live test also surfaced a bug — the work order pulled MSRP instead of vendor cost.

Accounting kept refining the ledger's edges. Dave moved to strip Location and Channel from the cost-adjustment template ("SKU + Lot should have the same cost across all locations and channels"), stood up a revenue-ledger sandbox for Prepared Hero, and confirmed credit-memo line items (Noel) plus credit-memo sync to QBO (Ash) land by early August.

Skills, Agents & the MCP

Two New Agents Clock In — and the Skill Strategy Gets a Reset

The AI Support Agent and Aurora go to work internally, while Harish reframes GoodDay's published skills as reference implementations, not products.
Sources: #project-ai, #mcp-launch-skills, #merchant-success

The headline agent is Hunter's AI Support Agent (see P.1): every Pylon ticket now gets an auto-drafted, knowledge-base-grounded answer posted for MSM review before anything reaches a merchant. Alongside it, Aurora joined the Merchant Success squad as a context-rich briefing assistant. Both sit atop Ser Rekonalot, which had its busiest triage week yet.

The strategic shift came from Harish, reflecting on Texas Standard: GoodDay should "reset the messaging around published skills" and position them as reference implementations rather than something customers use directly. The goal was always to get merchants building their own skills; leading with defaults makes them explore less. It's a sharper articulation of last issue's insight — the MCP wins when merchants co-create, not consume.

The team also caught a real edge in the write path. While spec'ing the split/reassign flow, Dave found the CSV block-splits import can create an already-closed shipment via a repository path that bypasses the service layer — "a create, not a transition." Merchants shouldn't be able to split or reassign onto a closed shipment via API or MCP, so a fix was ticketed to Ashwin across the endpoint, CSV upload, and MCP tool. Guardrails, before the agents get more autonomy.
