"Agent-Driven Operations" Becomes the Pitch; The Lead Conference Resonates

Kyle's NYC week reframes the GoodDay positioning around agents. Hencer pushes Kelley to add two more in-person events. The site is about to follow.
By the GTM Desk · Filed from #go-to-market and The Lead 2026

"At both breakfast and dinner — positioning GoodDay as the way you get into agent-driven operations is resonating hugely," Kyle Hency posted to #go-to-market Wednesday morning, after a New York week at The Lead 2026. He followed with a directive: "We should also begin to reframe the entire site experience along this line."

The positioning shift is the week's most consequential GTM story. Kyle and Kelley Flatley spent the conference working DTConnect meetings and the GoodDay breakfast invitation, with Meagan having pre-built one-pager prep for each prospect via Claude. Kelley returned from PTO Monday and flew to NYC the same day, missing the Sunday team dinner. "Crushed the programming/convo piece. Definitely left with some serious prospects!" she wrote Tuesday afternoon.

The follow-through includes more events, not fewer. Meagan challenged Kelley to add two additional in-person conferences/events in the next two months. Kyle's plan: skip the $8K Commerce Next entry fee but get one dinner in June and one in July with partners — focused on those who help with CPG. He's already calibrating: "Just be more scrappy and just send Kelley or me, likely."

Hencer also added an "agent-driven" lens to the HubSpot funnel. New deal stage: "qualified deal" sits between Scheduled Intro and the existing post-demo stages. Meagan's job to add it.

Beneath the positioning win, the week was punctuated by Kelley closing in on Guizio — a ~$50K deal and second Burch portfolio brand. Sticking point: Joor integration timeline. Kelley's ask to Wardell late Wednesday: "How is that currently slated and can we prioritize if we sign them?" Answer pending.

The Lead also produced an unexpected partnership inquiry. Codeture's Stefan — a long-time Fulfil implementation partner whose customers include Mejuri, Abbott Lyon, Wild, AKT London, Endy, and Casper — came through the GoodDay website to revisit GoodDay as an alternative to Fulfil. Meagan flagged the inquiry to Hencer Wednesday afternoon. See Page 3 for full coverage.

Codeture Inquires; Moselle Partnership Begins With Canopy

A long-time Fulfil implementation partner with a who's-who customer list comes back to GoodDay. Meanwhile, the demand-planning platform powering Canopy starts a formal integration.
Sourced from #partnerships and Gong (Evan Smith)

The most consequential inbound this week was Codeture. On Wednesday afternoon, Meagan flagged a website inquiry to Hencer from Stefan at codeture.nl. Stefan's pitch was direct: "Hey, we've been a long time Fulfil partner and have helped (and still work with) many Fulfil customers like Mejuri, Abbott Lyon, Wild, AKT London, Endy, Casper and many others. A few years ago, I investigated GoodDay as an alternative to Fulfil, but it was still early days back then. I am reconsidering Fulfil as the right choice for us to partner with and I would like to revisit where GoodDay stands and it it might be a better choice. Would it be possible to have a chat?"

The implementation-partner-network angle is new territory. Hencer hasn't engaged Stefan in years; the prior conversation was when the product was much earlier. The roll-call of Stefan's client list reads like a Fulfil case-study deck — Mejuri alone is a meaningful prize. Next step: Hencer to take the call.

Canopy finished their Moselle onboarding about a month ago. GoodDay is now starting a formal partnership; Canopy will be the first integration use case, handled via data warehouse connection and APIs once Canopy is live.

That puts pressure on the Canopy onboarding timeline. Evan's note to MS: "We will need to get Canopy live so we have data to flow into Moselle but wanted to get on y'all's radar well ahead." See Page 4 for the Canopy go-live thread.

Evan logged 10 partner calls across Monday and Friday — most short, scoping conversations. The Dwight Funding integration from last issue is still in motion behind the scenes; partner-side activity has been steady but quieter than the Codeture inquiry.
