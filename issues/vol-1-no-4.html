Kyle Reframes the Pitch: Co-Man, Returns & Partner APIs Lead The Webinar

Hencer asks Meagan to "deprecate the idea of MCP" from the public marketing motion. Co-Man Suite, Amazon FBA, SPS Commerce, B2B Returns, and June's Partner API launch take center stage. Internal MCP work moves to the AI Desk.
By the GTM Desk · Filed from #go-to-market

"Let's deprecate the idea of MCP," Kyle Hency wrote to Meagan Tuesday morning, just hours before her webinar prep deadline. The directive — counterintuitive given the last three issues' MCP-as-positioning energy — sharpens the public-facing pitch around four pillars: Co-Man Suite, the new Amazon FBA and SPS Commerce integrations, the already-shipped Wholesale/B2B Returns capabilities (Loop integration to follow in June), and the partner API release scheduled for June ("good for PLM integrations and B2B selling platforms like Joor").

The shift doesn't mean the team is backing away from MCP — far from it. Internal MCP work continues at full pace; see Page 6 for the full AI Desk roundup. But the customer-facing narrative gets a different center of gravity, and Meagan's webinar slides were rebuilt around it. The webinar pulled 31 registrants — mostly customers (Canopy, Clayton & Crume, Eby, Kenny Flowers, TomboyX, Hollow Socks, Auter, SaunaBox) and partners (ShipBob, Shopfusion, Crstl, TwoBoxes). Top external lead: James Jacobe at Seen, who Hencer had talked to last summer.

The Mini MCP, meanwhile, went out to the GTM team. Kyle's posted setup: a custom Claude connector pointing to mini-mcp.gooddaysoftware.com/stream, paired with a personal Claude Pro account. Hencer, Kelley, Evan, and Meagan are each connecting their own to use during prospect demos, with the production MCP staying inside the Merchant Success and Eng teams for now.

Meanwhile, the operational story of the week was nothing to do with messaging. Thursday morning brought app-wide slowness — Seth reported from Sierra Madre at 6:25 AM that page loads were taking forever; Dala flagged the same issue. By 7:05 AM Wardell had all of Eng on deck, with Macroscope flagging two suspect commits from the day prior. Kevin (on ATC) led the triage; James updated Kenny Flowers and Dala by 7:49 AM that the window appeared to have normalized. Performance returned by mid-morning, but David spent the day shifting all calls to focus on Lola's outstanding oversell situation, Prepared Hero, and a Bogey Bros inventory call.

Saturday's Branch deal scramble bookended the week. Kelley and the team had four days to put together a multi-entity, multi-currency, multi-warehouse fit story for a customer about to renew Extensiv at $80K/year. The team's response was to be radically transparent: Kelley's draft "where we land" email lays out gaps (WAC, multi-entity, complex BOMs) and proposes paths forward (Shopify Markets, building together via APIs/MCP, future-roadmap items). Sent today.

The skills package shipped a day after Wardell ran a structured audit comparing the Production MCP against the Mini MCP with his eval suite. Four specific feedback items came out of the audit:

  • Verified-query catalog — Enumerate semantic://model for the production MCP, or confirm which of 9 missing names should be hand-authored. This single answer turns 1 launch-ready Skill into 6.
  • Empty BQ marts for the Demo tenant — Specifically, is mart_inventory populated for merchant 35? vendor_overview reports 42 POs for one vendor that check_purchase_orders can't see.
  • PO Block fields on check_purchase_order_live — Confirm eta_method and shipment-linkage fields are returned (needed for the gd-supply-check Skill's root-cause classifier).
  • list_po_receipts draft filter — Tool description says the BQ mart only carries closed receipts; confirm whether status="draft" works against a different mart.

The structural insight from Harish, posted the prior Friday: "Drop the entire WMS CSV and let MCP figure out the discrepancy. That way it can run a more efficient query and return only the data that actually matters. Let Claude do the query work and make use of the Data Warehouse."

Internal AI scope continues to expand. Wardell's Wednesday note: "Need to add boat/air split / expedited analysis" and "need to add open WOs to the propose PO analysis/process". Lead times should drive incoterms dates, not ETA dates — a structural correction worth tracking.
