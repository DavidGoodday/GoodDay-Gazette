Revenue Ledger Reaches Production; A Ten-Release Week Pours The Decimal Foundation

The revenue ledger and reporting stack went live on the production store Wednesday — the milestone the accounting initiative has been building toward — while ten releases shipped and the first ledger table flipped to decimal precision.
By the Engineering Desk · Filed from #engineering, #initiative-accounting, and #merchant-success

The number the team has been chasing for a month finally moved. On Wednesday, June 18, Vishal Marocha confirmed the revenue ledger plus reporting is live in production on the prod store — a test order flowed all the way through to Sigma, and end-to-end testing is now underway. It is the headline ship of the accounting initiative, the surface that has dominated the project channels since the spring.

It did not arrive alone. The same week, Troy landed the LedgerEntry decimal-precision schema migration (#7133) — six-digit precision, and the first ledger table to flip from integers to decimals. The migration took roughly five hours to run in production. It sits on top of a broader decimal/fractional-quantity workstream that Tyler Lowe opened with an architecture decision record (#7098) and a sweep of backend, frontend, Kysely and OpenTelemetry decimal helpers. The team has flagged the obvious watch-item: rounding and precision behavior across every surface that reads quantity.

The release log tells the story of the pace. Ten versions shipped inside the window — v0.132.6 through a Saturday hotfix at v0.132.8, then v0.133.0–.3 across Monday and Tuesday, and a three-deploy Thursday that ended at v0.134.2. Sai cut the unified-recon hotfix at v0.134.1; Noel closed the week late Thursday with a returns/Loop and presale-fulfillment hotfix at v0.134.2. Rob Hatfield's v0.133.2 fixed sales-order foreign-currency export.

Underneath the wins, two infrastructure stories ran hot. The Google Sheets export pipeline — which pulls an entire dataset into memory before writing — kept crashing on large merchants; Rob drafted a keyset-paging fix (#7150), and Dave Wardell floated simply turning the adjustments export off. And after Paka's sheet formulas broke when new columns (Order Discount, Tax, S&H) landed, Wardell tasked Ash with building both a GitHub Action and a Macroscope review blocker to stop any code that changes an existing GD Sheets column from merging without sign-off.

The merchant-facing drama of the week belonged to Ten Thousand's end-of-season surplus sale, which stress-tested GoodDay's single-owner-per-SKU sync model when roughly 1,350 surplus variants auto-linked across reused product pages. Full coverage on Page 4.

Ten Thousand's Surplus Sale Stress-Tests The Single-Owner Sync Model

A surplus sale that reused SKUs across new product pages forced a 1,350-variant audit; the Pylon queue settled to 183; Seth came back from a week out.
Sourced from #merchant-success, #sers-tavern, Pylon, and Gong customer syncs (57)

Ten Thousand ran an end-of-season surplus sale (code JUNE26EOS) on secondary Shopify product pages that reused existing SKUs — and that collided directly with one of GoodDay's core rules: a SKU can have only one owner for inventory sync. Each new surplus variant auto-linked and threatened to take over sync from the original, which would freeze the original variant and open an oversell risk.

Matt Pham, working alongside Ser, audited the blast radius: roughly 1,350 surplus variants auto-linked correctly, with zero duplicates or conflicts, and produced a three-phase playbook to manage the sale safely. Wardell was openly skeptical of some of Ser's claims along the way — at one point calling a result "sounds made up" — and the thread pulled in Hunter, James, and River before it settled.

The companion problem ran in #critical-support: a duplicate-SKU situation was blocking inventory adjustments from pushing to Shopify. David's audit found 12,679 items with a single Shopify connection (bulk-fixable) versus 3,787 items with two-to-four connections — the tricky tail, where some items carry three duplicate-SKU pages plus a fourth non-SKU page that is the actually-linked one. A fix deployed to prod on 6/13; Hunter finished updating all connections by 6/15.

Pylon volume came in at 183 tickets opened across June 12–18, issue numbers #8028 through #8210 — down about 5% from last week's 193 and about 9% under the 202 peak. The themes were familiar: reconciliation discrepancies, Shopify/WMS/marketplace sync, PO-upload templates, NuOrder and Deposco integration status, and wholesale invoicing/QuickBooks.

On the calendar: Seth was OOO June 12–16 and returned Tuesday to spin up a renewal project for existing merchants. Canopy must be live by July 15 and wants to master Co-Man before go-live; a recorded session ran Monday.
