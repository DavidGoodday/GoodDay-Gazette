Sales · Above the Fold

Canopy Signs; April Closes With Ten Wins

Clayton & Crume and Mirror Palais verbal yeses round out a banner month for GTM as Hencer rallies the team toward CPG and multi-brand packaging.
By the GTM Desk · Filed from #go-to-market

"Canopy contract just came through — boom boom!" Kyle Hency posted to #go-to-market at 1:44 p.m. on Wednesday, capping a week in which GoodDay logged verbal commitments from three additional brands. Together with prior-week closures, the company expects to record ten new merchant wins for April — a personal-best month for the GTM team.

The Canopy paper followed a fast-moving solutioning push by Dave Wardell and Kelley Flatley to scope a workable approach to Canopy's incumbent fulfillment provider, Fullfill, and to confirm Simple Bundles handles their bundle catalog without unfurling at third-party sales channels.

Verbal yeses from Clayton & Crume (Mon., May 5) and Mirror Palais (Kelley's relationship) close out the month's pipeline. Kelley emailed Eric Best at Mirror Palais "an hour or two ago" Thursday afternoon awaiting paper.

Kyle has set the tone for May: scaling against CPG — where Evan Smith warns subscriptions will surface as a recurring requirement — and answering the multi-brand question. "We could build a mgmt portal that is pure reporting, across all the brands," Wardell suggested.

Meagan Herfkens Hency is testing a new website-visitor tracking tool, RB2B, with hits piped into #sales-leads. Doss has been spotted "enjoying our ROI Calculator." Both signals suggest the top of the funnel is moving.

Pre-sales is still the rough patch. Hencer has had to slow-roll TA3 after late-stage scope creep. The Beard Club's "1:many on Virtual Kits" inquiry was deemed solvable but unprioritized: "Couldn't imagine us solving before July unless it is really big," Wardell wrote.
