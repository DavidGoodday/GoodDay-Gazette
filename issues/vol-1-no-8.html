Quantities Go Fractional End-To-End; Loop Returns Goes Live At Duvin

A six-decimal migration becomes the week's biggest workstream and reaches the integration boundaries — while the first merchant flips live on GoodDay's native Loop Returns.
By the Engineering Desk · Filed from #engineering, #project-returns-management, and #project-inventory-precision

The number that defined the week was 6 — as in six digits of decimal precision. Over the weekend of June 19–21, Troy Lowe landed roughly nineteen PRs converting quantity fields across GoodDay from integers to Decimal(14,6) — DB schema, backend DTOs, AG Grid bulk editors, PDFs, and integration egress — capping the sweep with #7217 (enable + QA fractional entry). Ser's Monday Dev Digest called it the single biggest workstream concentration in recent memory, roughly half the window.

By midweek the work shifted to the hardest part of any precision project: the integration boundaries where GoodDay's fractional on-hand meets integer-only partners. Vishal Marocha and Troy shipped oversell-safe directional rounding at sales-channel egress (#7268), integer rounding of Shopify inventory-adjust deltas (#7248) and SO/Return quantities at the Trackstar boundary (#7275), and a Prisma Int-truncation fix that had been silently dropping sub-one quantities (#7261). A production int↔decimal "query bridge" (GD-11570) gates fractions per environment while the rollout completes.

It did not ship alone. On Wednesday afternoon Willy Husted announced that Duvin is live on GoodDay/Loop Returns — the first merchant on the platform's native returns integration — and within hours Noel confirmed returns were already flowing into GoodDay. Eight prod deploys shipped (v0.134.3 through v0.134.10), and a single 11.75 MB poison message wedged the prod worker for about thirty hours, redelivering every seven minutes, before Noel and Vishal raised the worker body limit to 16 MB (#7184). Pylon volume ticked back up to 199 tickets. Full coverage on Pages 4–6.
